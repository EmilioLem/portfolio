Things I forget "some" (more like every) time's

Written by Emilio Lemus

Let's be honest, none of us can remember everything we need, but some of us go further the basic needs, at a point when we have a complete list of how to do things we already do commonly. If you're like me, maybe you can use this compilation to get up to speed, and start learning quickly.

How to publish a game online, easily

You might be finishing a game, or doing common coding practices on a unity course, in which they ask you you share your work.

Â¿But how?

Well, it's easier than you thing, just follow these steps:

  1. Once in Unity, go to "File" -> "Build Settings..." and switch the platform to WebGL. If not listed, "Add open scenes" (maybe all, maybe just the ones you want).
  2. Look in the top bar for "window" -> "Package Manager", switch the top left selector to Unity registry and in the top right search bar, look for "WebGL Publisher. It autocompletes. Then install
  3. In the top bar, a new option should appear: "Publish". Inside it there's a single option. Click, then publish the scene, and it is VERY IMPORTANT that you select a folder made by yourself, outside the current unity project, otherwise it won't let you to publish automatically to the web.

That's it! It will open the game on unity play, the link of which you can share with your friends and family.

Later on, if you sign up with your Unity ID, you will find your latest games there.
